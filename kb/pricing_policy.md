# Pricing Policy

## Pricing Tiers

The platform currently supports three commercial tiers: Standard, Professional, and Enterprise.

The Standard plan is designed for small teams and includes core CRM automation, email triage, dashboard access, and standard support. The Professional plan includes higher usage limits, additional integrations, advanced analytics, and priority support. The Enterprise plan includes custom pricing, security review, premium SLA terms, dedicated onboarding, custom compliance documentation, and negotiated API limits.

## Non-Profit Discount

Registered non-profit organizations are eligible for a 30% discount on the Standard plan after verification. The discount applies to eligible seats only and does not automatically apply to Enterprise custom contracts unless approved by Sales leadership.

If a customer mentions that they are a registered non-profit, charity, 501(c)(3), NGO, educational organization, or community-service organization, the support agent should retrieve this policy and confirm eligibility. The agent should not promise the discount until documentation is verified.

## Pro-Rata Billing

When customers add seats mid-cycle, billing is calculated on a pro-rata basis for the remaining days in the billing period. The pro-rata charge applies only to the additional seats or upgraded capacity added during the current cycle.

If a customer upgrades from Standard to Professional or Enterprise mid-cycle, the billing team calculates the difference between the old tier and the new tier for the remaining days in the month. The customer should receive a clear explanation before the charge is applied.

## Enterprise Pricing

Enterprise pricing is custom and depends on seat count, usage volume, security requirements, SLA terms, integrations, and support commitments. Any opportunity above 100 seats or above $50,000 annual contract value should be routed to Sales.

## Pricing Objection Handling

When a customer says the product is expensive, the agent should first understand whether the objection is about total cost, seat count, contract term, or feature value. For high-value or churn-risk accounts, the agent should escalate to the account owner instead of offering unauthorized discounts.

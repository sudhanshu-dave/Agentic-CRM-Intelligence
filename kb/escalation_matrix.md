# Escalation Matrix

## Legal Threats

Emails mentioning legal action, cease and desist, trademark violation, formal correspondence, contractual breach, renewal on hold due to incident, or legal review must be escalated to the Legal team immediately.

Recommended owner: Legal Operations  
Priority: Critical

## Security Incidents

Emails mentioning ransomware, data breach, suspicious login, exfiltrated records, valid credentials used by unknown actor, dark web publication threat, or payment demand must be escalated to the Security team immediately.

Recommended owner: Security Incident Response  
Priority: Critical  
Auto-reply allowed: No

## GDPR and Privacy Requests

Formal GDPR, data portability, deletion, export, privacy, or data subject access requests must be routed to the Privacy or Legal team.

Recommended owner: Privacy Operations  
Priority: Critical or High depending on legal wording  
Auto-reply allowed: Only approved acknowledgement, never generic support reply

## Reputation Crises

Emails threatening negative reviews, public posts, social media escalation, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Twitter, press, or investor attention should be escalated to Customer Success or Support leadership.

Recommended owner: Customer Success Lead  
Priority: High

## VIP Churn Risk

High-value customers, Enterprise customers, customers with renewal risk, and customers threatening cancellation should be escalated to Customer Success.

Recommended owner: Account Owner or Customer Success Manager  
Priority: High

## Product Bugs

Mission-critical bugs, data corruption, failed imports, crashes, login failures, and production-impacting defects should create an Engineering ticket.

Recommended owner: Engineering Support  
Priority: High or Critical depending on impact

## Internal Emails

Internal emails from internal.com, mycompany.com, company.com, GitHub build alerts, or system notifications should be routed to the internal inbox and should not trigger customer auto-replies.

## Spam

SEO pitches, fake inheritance messages, cold outreach, suspicious marketing offers, and generic sales spam should be ignored and should not receive auto-replies.

# Refund Policy

## Standard Refund Rule

The standard policy is that refunds are not available after 14 days from the original purchase or renewal date. Customers may be eligible for credits, service extensions, or goodwill offers depending on account history and issue severity.

## Exceptions

Exceptions may be considered when there is a verified platform failure, billing error, duplicate charge, severe service disruption, or misleading product communication. Exceptions require approval from Support leadership or Billing operations.

## Credits vs Refunds

Support agents should prefer credits or service extensions before offering direct refunds. Direct refunds should not be promised without approval.

## Churn Retention Playbook

If a customer threatens to cancel, churn, leave a negative review, or post publicly on social platforms, the agent should escalate the case to a human account owner. The agent should retrieve the customer thread history, identify unresolved prior messages, and prepare a retention brief.

Possible retention actions include priority support, billing credit review, temporary service credit, escalation to engineering, or direct account manager outreach.

## Public Review Threats

If a customer mentions G2, Trustpilot, Capterra, Twitter, social media, public review, or public complaint, the issue should be considered reputation-sensitive. The agent should not send a generic auto-reply. It should escalate with high priority and include a summary of the customer's history.

## Chatbot Misinformation

If the customer says an AI chatbot or support bot gave incorrect refund information, the agent should acknowledge the discrepancy carefully, retrieve the actual refund policy, escalate for human review, and avoid language that admits legal liability.

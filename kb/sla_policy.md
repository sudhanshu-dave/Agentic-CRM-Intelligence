# SLA Policy

## Uptime Commitment

Enterprise customers may be covered by a 99.9% monthly uptime service level agreement if their contract includes SLA terms. Standard and Professional customers may receive best-effort support unless an SLA addendum exists.

## P0 Incident Definition

A P0 incident means a production system is unavailable, business-critical workflows are blocked, or the customer is experiencing severe financial or operational impact. Examples include production outage, data unavailability, failed authentication across many users, or inability to process business-critical operations.

## Response Time Targets

For P0 incidents, the first response target is 30 minutes for covered Enterprise customers. Engineering and support teams should open an incident channel immediately and provide periodic updates until recovery.

For P1 incidents, the first response target is 4 business hours. For P2 and lower, normal support response times apply.

## RCA Delivery

For P0 incidents, a root cause analysis report should be delivered within 24 hours after incident resolution when contractually required. The RCA should include incident timeline, customer impact, root cause, corrective actions, and prevention measures.

## SLA Credit Calculation

SLA credits are calculated based on downtime duration, monthly subscription value, and the customer contract. Support agents must not calculate or promise exact credits unless the account status and contract terms are verified.

If a customer requests SLA credits, the agent should acknowledge the request, retrieve the incident details, escalate to the account owner, and reference the SLA credit review process.

## Legal or Renewal Risk

If a customer mentions legal review, formal correspondence, renewal on hold, breach, damages, or contractual violation, the agent must flag the issue for Legal and escalate to a human immediately. The agent may draft an empathetic holding response but must not admit liability.
